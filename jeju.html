The Island of the Gods

Formed by volcanic eruptions over 2 million years ago, Jeju Island is Korea's premier natural escape. Designated a UNESCO World Natural Heritage site, its dramatic landscapes range from lava tubes to subtropical forests.

Hallasan: Korea's Tallest Peak

The dormant volcano at Jeju's center offers hikes through ecosystems that change with every kilometer of elevation. The 7-hour round trip to the summit passes through alpine forests and volcanic craters.

For a gentler experience, explore the Eorimok or Yeongsil trails that showcase Hallasan's breathtaking valleys.

Seongsan Ilchulbong Sunrise Peak

This dramatic tuff cone rising from the sea is Jeju's most iconic landmark. The 30-minute hike to the crater rim rewards early risers with spectacular sunrise views over the Pacific.

Nearby, the women divers (haenyeo) of Jeju demonstrate their traditional free-diving techniques, a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Island Explorer's Guide

When to Visit

April for cherry blossoms, June for hydrangeas, September for mild weather and harvest festivals.

Getting Around

Rent a car for the best access to remote areas. Public buses are available but less convenient.

Local Flavors

Try black pork BBQ, fresh abalone, and hallabong oranges - Jeju's signature citrus fruit.
