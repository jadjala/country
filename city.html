The Rhythm of Seoul

Seoul is a city of contrasts, where 600-year-old palaces stand shoulder-to-shoulder with futuristic skyscrapers. This metropolis of nearly 10 million moves at a thrilling pace, yet maintains pockets of serene tradition.

Gangnam: Korea's Beverly Hills

The glitzy district of Gangnam embodies Seoul's economic miracle. By day, corporate warriors hustle in glass towers. By night, the streets pulse with neon from luxury boutiques and underground cocktail bars.

Don't miss the COEX Aquarium or the Starfield Library - architectural marvels that showcase Korea's design-forward mentality.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Step back in time among the curved tiled roofs of Bukchon's traditional hanok houses. This preserved neighborhood offers stunning views of modern Seoul framed by 14th-century architecture.

Many hanoks now house tea shops and craft studios - perfect for experiencing Korean aesthetics at their most refined.

Urban Explorer's Checklist

Transport

Seoul's subway is the world's most technologically advanced. Get a T-money card for seamless travel across all lines.

Food

Try gopchang (grilled intestines) in Jongno or modern Korean fusion in Itaewon's multicultural district.

Culture

Visit Dongdaemun Design Plaza at night when its futuristic curves glow against the skyline.
